💧 Wet = LIQUID COOLING

Liquid cooling is no longer optional — it's mandatory. Air cooling cannot remove heat fast enough from modern chips. Liquid cooling uses water or dielectric fluids to absorb and transfer heat up to 4,000 times more efficiently than air.

  • Direct-to-Chip cooling: fluid circulates through cold plates on top of chips.
  • Immersion cooling: servers are submerged in non-conductive fluid.
  • Google, Microsoft, and Amazon are switching entire data centers to liquid cooling.
  • Liquid cooling reduces PUE (Power Usage Effectiveness) from 1.5 to as low as 1.05.
